Capitalism

We currently live in a world dominated by a social and economic system which assumes competitive markets, private ownership of the means of production, infinite expansion through imperialism, and wage-labour: Capitalism. It is a system predicated on the assumption that every individual makes the most rational choice in his interaction with the market, to achieve equilibrium. Its proponents argue that this system of so-called free markets breeds freedom, innovation, and efficient allocation of resources, right as we observe authoritarian developments in the western hemisphere, manufactured consent for expansionist wars, incredible surges in wealth inequality and poverty, as well as a seemingly unsolvable and rapidly incoming climate crisis, on top of huge distribution problems regarding food and housing. So-called right-wing 'libertarians' even go as far as to argue that state intervention is the root cause of those problems, and that a laissez-faire capitalist economy would do much better to improve material conditions for everyone. Their ideas assume an 'invisible hand' guiding the markets, a principle that is far from an effective truth. Many are the reasons for which Capitalism, and by extension attempts at its free-market forms, is a mediocre socio-economic system. Let's take some time to critique capitalism, point out its flaws and rebut goofy right-winger bullshit.
